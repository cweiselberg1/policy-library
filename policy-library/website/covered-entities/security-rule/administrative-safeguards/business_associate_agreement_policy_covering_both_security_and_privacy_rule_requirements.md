4  PURPOSE OF THIS POLICY

To make sure [Company] only shares protected health information (PHI) with outside vendors, contractors, or partners (“business associates”) who have signed a HIPAA-compliant contract promising to safeguard that information.

5  POLICY STATEMENTS

6  [COMPANY] WILL ONLY SHARE PHI WITH A BUSINESS ASSOCIATE AFTER BOTH SIDES HAVE SIGNED A BUSINESS ASSOCIATE AGREEMENT (OR BAA).

The BAA must contain language stating that the business associate:

Can only use or share PHI as allowed by law or the contract.

Must keep PHI secure, use proper safeguards, and implement technical safeguards for electronic PHI (or ePHI).

7  MUST TELL [COMPANY] RIGHT AWAY IF PHI IS USED OR SHARED BY MISTAKE, OR IF A BREACH HAPPENS.

Must make any subcontractor/vendors agree to the same rules.

Has to return or destroy PHI when the work ends, if possible.

8  [COMPANY] CAN END THE AGREEMENT IF THERE IS A SERIOUS HIPAA VIOLATION THAT ISN’T FIXED.

9  PROCEDURES

10  IDENTIFY ANY VENDOR OR PARTNER NEEDING ACCESS TO PHI BEFORE SUCH WORK BEGINS.

Sign a BAA before sharing any PHI.

11  KEEP TRACK OF ALL BAAS AND REVIEW THEM YEARLY FOR COMPLIANCE.

Keep copies of BAAs for at least six years after work ends.

12  ENFORCEMENT

Breaking this policy can lead to disciplinary action, up to and including termination. If you have a question regarding this policy, please be sure to ask your Compliance Officer.
