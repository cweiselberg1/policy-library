In compliance with 45 CFR §164.308(a)(7)(ii)(A)-(E)

1  PURPOSE OF THIS POLICY

To establish and implement policies and procedures for responding to emergencies or other occurrences (such as fire, vandalism, system failure, and natural disaster) that damage systems containing electronic protected health information (ePHI), in accordance with the HIPAA Security Rule administrative safeguards.

2  POLICY STATEMENTS

3  DATA BACKUP PLAN

4  [COMPANY] WILL ESTABLISH AND IMPLEMENT PROCEDURES TO CREATE AND MAINTAIN RETRIEVABLE EXACT COPIES OF EPHI.

Backups of all ePHI will be performed [daily/weekly/as appropriate] and stored securely in a manner that ensures retrievability in the event of a data loss incident.

Backup media and systems will be protected with appropriate physical and technical safeguards to prevent unauthorized access or alteration.

5  DISASTER RECOVERY PLAN

6  PROCEDURES WILL BE ESTABLISHED AND IMPLEMENTED TO RESTORE ANY LOSS OF EPHI DATA.

The disaster recovery plan will specify roles, responsibilities, and actions to be taken to restore ePHI and critical systems as quickly as possible following a disaster or system failure.

The plan will include contact information for key personnel, vendors, and emergency services.

7  EMERGENCY MODE OPERATION PLAN

The organization will establish and implement procedures to enable the continuation of critical business processes for the protection of the security of ePHI while operating in emergency mode.

The plan will identify essential personnel, critical systems, and processes that must be maintained to protect ePHI during and after an emergency.

8  ACCESS TO EPHI DURING EMERGENCY OPERATIONS WILL BE LIMITED TO AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL ONLY.

9  TESTING AND REVISION PROCEDURES

The contingency plans (including backup, disaster recovery, and emergency mode operation) will be tested and revised periodically (at least annually or after significant changes to systems or operations).

Results of tests and any identified deficiencies will be documented, and the plans will be updated accordingly.

10  RESPONSIBILITIES

The Compliance Officer is responsible for the development, implementation, and maintenance of this policy.

All workforce members are required to comply with this policy and participate in training and testing as directed.

11  DOCUMENTATION AND REVIEW

All procedures, test results, revisions, and criticality analyses must be documented and retained in accordance with HIPAA documentation requirements.

This policy will be reviewed at least annually and updated as necessary to reflect changes in operations, technology, or regulations.
