Disaster Recovery PLAN

P O L I C Y

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

In Compliance With 45 CFR §164.308(a)(7)(ii)(B)

1  PLAN ACTIVATION

2  ACTIVATION CRITERIA

The DRP is activated in response to events that result in the loss, compromise, or unavailability of ePHI or critical systems, including but not limited to:

Natural disasters (fire, flood, hurricane, earthquake)

3  CYBERATTACKS (MALWARE, RANSOMWARE, DATA BREACH)

System or hardware failure

Vandalism, terrorism, or other criminal acts.

4  AUTHORITY TO ACTIVATE

The HIPAA Security Officer, or designated alternate, is responsible for declaring a disaster and activating the DRP.

5  ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

6  CONTACT INFORMATION FOR ALL KEY PERSONNEL AND VENDORS MUST BE MAINTAINED AND REVIEWED QUARTERLY.

7  DATA BACKUP AND RESTORATION

8  DATA BACKUP

All ePHI is backed up according to the Data Backup Plan (see separate document), with backups stored securely offsite and tested regularly for integrity and retrievability46.

9  RESTORATION PRIORITY

Restoration of data and systems must follow a documented order of priority:

ePHI databases and clinical systems

Communication systems (email, phones)

Billing and scheduling systems

Other business-critical applications4.

The criticality of each system is determined by the Application and Data Criticality Analysis.

10  RESTORATION PROCEDURES

Retrieve the latest verified backup from secure storage.

Restore ePHI and critical systems to designated recovery hardware or cloud platforms.

Validate data integrity and system functionality before resuming normal operations.

Document all restoration activities, including time, personnel, and outcomes375.

11  COMMUNICATION AND COORDINATION

Notify all affected staff, leadership, and business associates of the disaster and recovery status.

Communicate with vendors (e.g., IT support, cloud providers, utilities) using up-to-date contact lists.

Provide regular updates to stakeholders until full restoration is achieved4.

12  DOCUMENTATION AND REPORTING

13  LOG ALL SYSTEM OUTAGES, FAILURES, AND DATA LOSS INCIDENTS.

Document each step of the recovery process, including decisions, actions, and validation results.

14  SUBMIT A POST-INCIDENT REPORT TO THE HIPAA SECURITY OFFICER FOR REVIEW AND RETENTION375.

15  TRAINING AND AWARENESS

16  ALL WORKFORCE MEMBERS WITH DRP RESPONSIBILITIES MUST RECEIVE ANNUAL TRAINING ON THE PLAN.

17  THE DRP MUST BE ACCESSIBLE TO ALL RELEVANT STAFF, WITH COPIES STORED BOTH ONSITE AND OFFSITE5.

18  PLAN MAINTENANCE AND REVIEW

The DRP is reviewed and updated at least annually, or after any significant change to systems, personnel, or business processes.

Test the DRP at least annually through tabletop exercises or simulations, and update based on lessons learned745.

19  PLAN STORAGE

Maintain current copies of the DRP at:

The primary business location

The designated offsite backup location

20  WITH THE HIPAA SECURITY OFFICER AND IT MANAGER75

21  REGULATORY LANGUAGE REFERENCE

“Establish (and implement as needed) procedures to restore any loss of data.” — 45 CFR §164.308(a)(7)(ii)(B).
