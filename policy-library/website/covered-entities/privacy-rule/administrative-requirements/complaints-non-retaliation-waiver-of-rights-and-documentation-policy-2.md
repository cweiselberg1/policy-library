Complaints, Non-Retaliation, Waiver of Rights, and Documentation

P O L I C Y

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

In Compliance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.530(d)-(h), 45 CFR § 164.316(b), & 45 CFR § 164.530(j)

1  PURPOSE OF THIS POLICY

To ensure that all individuals have the right to file HIPAA-related complaints regarding [Company]'s privacy or security practices, that those complaints are handled professionally and documented appropriately, and to safeguard against retaliation or required relinquishment of rights for exercising HIPAA protections.

2  POLICY STATEMENT

[Company] will accept, review, and document all complaints related to HIPAA compliance. Individuals will not be subject to any retaliation for filing a complaint or exercising their HIPAA rights, nor will they be required to waive any of their rights as a precondition of service.

3  COMPLAINT SUBMISSION AND HANDLING

Any person (patient, staff, workforce member, or partner) may submit a complaint regarding the handling of PHI, HIPAA violations, or any privacy/security concern.

4  COMPLAINTS MAY BE SUBMITTED VERBALLY OR IN WRITING TO THE DESIGNATED COMPLIANCE OFFICER.

Complaint forms and instructions will be made easily available.

The Compliance Officer will review and investigate each complaint promptly and fairly, maintaining confidentiality to the extent possible.

5  NON-RETALIATION

No individual will suffer intimidation, threats, coercion, discrimination, or retaliation for:

6  FILING A PRIVACY OR SECURITY COMPLAINT.

7  PARTICIPATING IN AN INVESTIGATION, COMPLIANCE REVIEW, OR HEARING.

Opposing any act or practice viewed as a HIPAA violation.

8  WAIVER OF RIGHTS

No individual will be required to waive HIPAA rights as a condition for receiving treatment, payment, or benefits.

9  DOCUMENTATION AND RECORDKEEPING

10  [COMPANY] WILL MAINTAIN DOCUMENTATION OF

All complaints received and their resolution/outcome.

All related investigations and corrective actions.

11  THIS POLICY AND ANY RELATED PROCEDURES OR COMMUNICATIONS.

Records will be retained for at least six years from the date of creation or the date when they last were in effect, whichever is later.

12  TRAINING AND AWARENESS

Workforce members will be trained on how to handle complaints, respect non-retaliation, and maintain proper recordkeeping on an annual basis.

Individuals will be made aware of their rights to complain and the process to file a complaint.

13  ENFORCEMENT

Employees found in breach of these provisions (e.g., retaliating, requiring waiver of rights, or failing to document complaints properly) are subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination.

14  REVIEW AND REVISION

This policy will be reviewed annually and updated as necessary to ensure ongoing compliance with HIPAA regulations and organizational practices.
