In compliance with 45 CFR §164.530, 45 CFR §164.530(a), 45 CFR §164.530(a)(1), 45 CFR §164.530(a)(1)(i), 45 CFR §164.530(a)(1)(ii), & 45 CFR §164.530(a)(2)

1  PURPOSE OF THIS POLICY

To ensure [Company] implements and maintains appropriate administrative requirements for the protection of protected health information (PHI), including the designation of responsible personnel, as required by the HIPAA Privacy Rule.

2  POLICY STATEMENTS

3  DESIGNATION OF COMPLIANCE OFFICER

4  CFR §164.530(A)(1)(I)

[Company] will designate a Compliance Officer who is responsible for the development, implementation, and oversight of the organization’s HIPAA privacy policies and procedures.

The Compliance Officer will serve as the primary point of contact for all compliance-related matters and ensure ongoing compliance with the HIPAA Privacy Rule, HIPAA Security Rule, HiTECH Act, and OMNIBUS.

5  DESIGNATION OF CONTACT PERSON OR OFFICE

6  CFR §164.530(A)(1)(II)

7  [COMPANY] WILL DESIGNATE A CONTACT PERSON OR OFFICE RESPONSIBLE FOR

8  RECEIVING COMPLAINTS REGARDING THE ORGANIZATION’S PRIVACY PRACTICES OR POTENTIAL VIOLATIONS.

Providing individuals with further information about matters covered by the Notice of Privacy Practices (NPP) as required under 45 CFR §164.520.

9  DOCUMENTATION OF PERSONNEL DESIGNATIONS

10  CFR §164.530(A)(2)

The names and titles of the designated Compliance Officer and Contact Person or Office will be documented and retained in accordance with HIPAA’s documentation requirements.

Documentation will be updated promptly whenever personnel changes occur.

11  ADMINISTRATIVE SAFEGUARDS

12  CFR §164.530

[Organization Name] will implement appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to reasonably protect PHI from intentional or unintentional use or disclosure in violation of HIPAA standards, and to limit incidental uses or disclosures made pursuant to otherwise permitted or required uses or disclosures.

13  THESE SAFEGUARDS WILL BE REVIEWED AND UPDATED AS NECESSARY TO ENSURE ONGOING PROTECTION OF PHI.

14  POLICY IMPLEMENTATION AND REVIEW

This policy, and all related procedures, will be reviewed at least annually and updated as necessary to reflect changes in law, regulation, or organizational practices.

All workforce members will be informed of the designated Compliance Officer and Contact Person or Office, and trained on their responsibilities regarding PHI.

15  RESPONSIBILITIES

Compliance Officer: Oversee HIPAA privacy and security compliance, develop and implement policies, respond to both security-related and privacy-related inquiries.

16  CONTACT PERSON/OFFICE: RECEIVE COMPLAINTS, PROVIDE INFORMATION ABOUT PRIVACY PRACTICES, AND ASSIST WITH INDIVIDUAL RIGHTS REQUESTS.

17  ALL WORKFORCE MEMBERS: COMPLY WITH THIS POLICY AND REPORT ANY CONCERNS OR COMPLAINTS REGARDING PRIVACY PRACTICES TO THE COMPLIANCE OFFICER.

18  DOCUMENTATION AND RETENTION

All personnel designations and related documentation will be maintained for at least six years, as required by HIPAA.

19  REVIEW AND REVISION

This policy will be reviewed annually or upon significant changes to facility operations, technology, or applicable regulations.
