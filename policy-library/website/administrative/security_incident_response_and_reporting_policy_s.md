Security Incident Response and Reporting

P O L I C Y

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

In compliance with  45 CFR §164.308(a)(6)

1  PURPOSE OF THIS POLICY

To establish and implement policies and procedures for the identification, reporting, response, documentation, and mitigation of security incidents involving ePHI, as required by the HIPAA Security Rule.

2  DEFINITIONS

Security Incident: The attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations in an information system containing ePHI.

3  POLICY STATEMENTS

4  IDENTIFICATION AND REPORTING

All workforce members must immediately report any known or suspected security incident involving ePHI to the designated Security Officer or their designee.

5  SECURITY INCIDENTS MAY INCLUDE, BUT ARE NOT LIMITED TO

6  UNAUTHORIZED ACCESS OR ATTEMPTED ACCESS TO EPHI SYSTEMS

Loss or theft of devices containing ePHI

Malware or ransomware infections

Unusual system activity or performance

Improper disclosure or modification of ePHI

7  RESPONSE PROCEDURES

8  UPON RECEIVING A REPORT OF A SECURITY INCIDENT, THE SECURITY OFFICER WILL

9  LOG THE INCIDENT IN THE SECURITY INCIDENT REPORT LOG.

10  INVESTIGATE THE INCIDENT TO DETERMINE ITS NATURE, SCOPE, AND IMPACT.

11  CONTAIN AND MITIGATE THE INCIDENT TO PREVENT FURTHER UNAUTHORIZED ACCESS OR DAMAGE.

12  PRESERVE EVIDENCE AS APPROPRIATE FOR POTENTIAL ANALYSIS OR LEGAL PROCEEDINGS.

13  DOCUMENTATION

14  ALL SECURITY INCIDENTS MUST BE DOCUMENTED, INCLUDING

15  DATE AND TIME OF INCIDENT AND DISCOVERY

16  DESCRIPTION OF THE INCIDENT

Individuals involved or affected

Actions taken in response

Outcome and follow-up measures

17  NOTIFICATION

If the incident constitutes a breach under HIPAA or other applicable law, the Security Officer will ensure notification is made to:

Affected individuals

The Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)

Other regulatory authorities as required

18  REVIEW AND MITIGATION

19  FOLLOWING EACH INCIDENT, A POST-INCIDENT REVIEW WILL BE CONDUCTED TO

Assess the effectiveness of the response

Identify root causes and contributing factors

Recommend and implement corrective actions to prevent recurrence

20  WORKFORCE TRAINING

21  ALL WORKFORCE MEMBERS WILL RECEIVE PERIODIC TRAINING ON

22  RECOGNIZING POTENTIAL SECURITY INCIDENTS

23  PROPER PROCEDURES FOR REPORTING INCIDENTS

24  THEIR RESPONSIBILITIES UNDER THIS POLICY

25  ENFORCEMENT

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract, in accordance with the organization’s sanction policy.

26  REVIEW

This policy will be reviewed at least annually and updated as necessary to reflect changes in regulations, technology, or organizational practices.
