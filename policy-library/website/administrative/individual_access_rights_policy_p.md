To fulfill HIPAA’s Right of Access (45 CFR §164.524) by:

1  PURPOSE OF THIS POLICY

2  GRANTING INDIVIDUALS PROMPT ACCESS TO THEIR PHI.

3  DEFINING PROCEDURES FOR REQUESTING, PROCESSING, AND DELIVERING PHI.

4  ENSURING STAFF ADHERENCE TO ACCESS PROTOCOLS AND DOCUMENTATION STANDARDS.

Individual Rights

Individuals have the right to:

Access PHI: Request to inspect or obtain copies of PHI in designated record sets (e.g., medical records, billing data).

Request Confidential Communications: Specify preferred contact methods (e.g., email, alternate address) for PHI disclosures. Reasonable requests must be accommodated without requiring justification.

Restrict Disclosures: Ask for limitations on PHI uses/disclosures, though [Organization Name] may deny non-mandatory restrictions if impractical.

5  REQUEST PROCEDURES

Submission:

Requests must be submitted in writing via [specify form/method, e.g., access request form, secure portal].

Include the individual’s name, contact details, PHI description, and preferred delivery format (e.g., paper, electronic).

Verification:

Identity must be validated using [specify method, e.g., government ID, security questions] to prevent unauthorized disclosures.

Processing Timelines

Fulfillment: Provide access within 30 days of request receipt. One 30-day extension is permitted with written notice explaining the delay.

Delivery: PHI provided in the requested format (if feasible) or mutually agreed alternative. Fees limited to labor/supplies costs, with a fee schedule provided upfront.

6  DENIALS AND REVIEWS

Grounds for Denial:

Psychotherapy notes; information compiled for legal proceedings; PHI exempted by law.

Denials must be issued in writing within 30 days, citing HIPAA basis and appeal rights.
