In Compliance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.308(a)(2), 45 CFR § 164.308(a)(1), & 45 CFR § 164.530(a)

1  PURPOSE OF THIS POLICY

To ensure [Company] implements required administrative safeguards, assigns HIPAA compliance responsibilities to specific individuals, and documents overall privacy and security program leadership and accountability as required by the HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules.

2  POLICY STATEMENT

[Company] is committed to maintaining an effective HIPAA compliance program. The organization will designate qualified individuals as Privacy and Security Officers, establish clear roles and responsibilities for HIPAA compliance, and implement administrative safeguards and procedures to protect protected health information (PHI) and electronic PHI (ePHI).

3  PERSONNEL DESIGNATION AND ASSIGNMENT

4  COMPLIANCE OFFICER

[Company] will designate a Compliance Officer responsible for the development, implementation, and oversight of privacy and security policies and procedures, workforce training, and compliance with HIPAA Privacy and Security Rule requirements.

Assignment of responsibilities may be held by different individuals or by a single responsible individual, depending on [Company]'s size and structure.

5  ADMINISTRATIVE SAFEGUARDS

Administrative duties include:

6  DEVELOPMENT, DOCUMENTATION, AND IMPLEMENTATION OF HIPAA POLICIES AND PROCEDURES.

Ongoing assessment and management of risks to PHI and ePHI.

7  TRAINING OF WORKFORCE MEMBERS ON HIPAA REQUIREMENTS AND COMPANY POLICIES.

Evaluating and updating policies in response to significant changes in operations, law, or technology.

8  SANCTIONING WORKFORCE MEMBERS WHO FAIL TO COMPLY WITH HIPAA POLICIES.

Ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of PHI/ePHI.

9  CLEAR DOCUMENTATION OF ALL POLICIES, PROCEDURES, AND UPDATES SHALL BE MAINTAINED.

10  RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE COMPLIANCE OFFICER

11  COMPLIANCE OFFICER SHALL

12  MONITOR AND DOCUMENT PRIVACY COMPLIANCE EFFORTS.

13  SERVE AS THE CONTACT PERSON FOR PRIVACY-RELATED COMPLAINTS AND INQUIRIES.

14  ORGANIZE AND DOCUMENT PRIVACY TRAINING AND COMMUNICATION.

15  OVERSEE PATIENT RIGHTS REQUESTS (ACCESS, AMENDMENT, RESTRICTION, ETC.).

Oversee risk assessments and mitigation strategies.

Maintain and monitor technical and physical safeguards.

16  RESPOND TO AND DOCUMENT SECURITY INCIDENTS OR BREACHES.

17  ENSURE REGULAR REVIEWS OF ACCESS CONTROLS AND AUDIT LOGS.

18  DOCUMENTATION

Written records of all policy documents, personnel designations, and reviews shall be retained for at least six (6) years from their creation or last effective date.

Documentation confirming the designation of Compliance Officers will be included in the HIPAA compliance program records.

19  ENFORCEMENT

Failure to comply with these policies or the responsibilities assigned may result in corrective action, up to and including termination.

20  REVIEW AND REVISION

This policy will be reviewed annually and updated as necessary to ensure ongoing compliance with HIPAA regulations and organizational practices.
