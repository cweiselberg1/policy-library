2  PURPOSE OF THIS POLICY

To ensure [Company] provides comprehensive, ongoing training to all workforce members regarding the privacy and security of protected health information (PHI), in compliance with HIPAA Rules.

3  POLICY STATEMENTS

4  PRIVACY RULE TRAINING

All workforce members must receive training on [Company]'s policies and procedures regarding PHI, as necessary and appropriate for their roles.

5  TRAINING MUST BE PROVIDED

6  TO EACH WORKFORCE MEMBER BY THE COMPLIANCE DATE OR START OF EMPLOYMENT.

To all new workforce members within 90 days after joining.

To current workforce members whenever a material change in policies or procedures affects their functions, within a reasonable period after the change becomes effective.

7  TRAINING MUST BE DOCUMENTED, INCLUDING DATES, CONTENT, AND ATTENDANCE RECORDS.

8  SECURITY AWARENESS AND TRAINING

[Company] will implement a security awareness and training program for all workforce members, including management, to prevent, detect, and respond to security threats to ePHI.

The program will include, at a minimum:

9  SECURITY REMINDERS: PERIODIC SECURITY UPDATES AND REMINDERS.

10  PROTECTION FROM MALICIOUS SOFTWARE: PROCEDURES FOR GUARDING AGAINST, DETECTING, AND REPORTING MALICIOUS SOFTWARE.

11  LOG-IN MONITORING: PROCEDURES FOR MONITORING LOG-IN ATTEMPTS AND REPORTING DISCREPANCIES.

12  PASSWORD MANAGEMENT: PROCEDURES FOR CREATING, CHANGING, AND SAFEGUARDING PASSWORDS.

13  TRAINING WILL BE UPDATED AS NEEDED TO ADDRESS NEW THREATS, TECHNOLOGIES, OR REGULATORY CHANGES.

14  ONGOING AND REFRESHER TRAINING

Refresher training will be provided at least annually and whenever there are significant changes in law, technology, or organizational practices.

Additional training will be provided if a workforce member's job duties change in a way that affects access to PHI or ePHI.

15  SANCTIONS FOR NON-COMPLIANCE

Workforce members who do not complete required training or who violate privacy and security policies will be subject to the organization's sanctions policy. Disciplinary actions may include, but are not limited to, corrective counseling, suspension, or termination of employment.

Documentation

All training activities, including content, attendance, and dates, will be documented and retained for six years, as required by HIPAA.

Responsibilities

16  PRIVACY OFFICER/SECURITY OFFICER: RESPONSIBLE FOR DEVELOPING, IMPLEMENTING, AND OVERSEEING THE TRAINING PROGRAM.

17  SUPERVISORS: ENSURE WORKFORCE MEMBERS COMPLETE REQUIRED TRAINING.

18  WORKFORCE MEMBERS: ATTEND AND PARTICIPATE IN ALL REQUIRED TRAINING SESSIONS AND COMPLY WITH ALL RELATED POLICIES AND PROCEDURES.

19  REVIEW
